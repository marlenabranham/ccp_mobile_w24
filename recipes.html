Double Chocolate Chip Cookies

Orgin: Michigan

Source: Family Recipe

Category: Dessert

My daughter learned to make these cookies at a baking camp at Zingermanns and has tweaked the recipe to fit the taste buds of her siblings. They are extremely sugary so the salt helps to balance it. Note, these cookies are best eaten very quickly.

Recipe Ingredients

  • unsaled butter, softened
  • cups white sugar
  • eggs
  • vanilla extract
  • unsweetened chocolate, melted
  • all-purpose flour
  • teaspoon baking soda
  • teaspoon salt
  • cup semisweet chocolate chips

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, and salt
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until combined
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips
  7. Drop by rounded teaspoonfuls onto a greased cookie sheet
  8. Bake at 350 degrees for 8-10 minutes
  9. Remove to a wire rack to cool

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

Orgin: American

Source: Delish

Category: Dessert

This is a tried-and-tested recipe that I have used numerous times for making the perfect chocolate chip banana bread! Not only does it help utilize the bananas that have slowly overripened at the back of the fridge, it ends up creating an incredibly delicious, and relatively healthy, dessert.

Recipe Ingredients

  • cooking spray
  • all-purpose flour
  • baking soda
  • kosher salt
  • unsalted butter, softened
  • granulated sugar
  • large eggs
  • ripe bananas, mashed
  • vanilla extract
  • chocolate chips
  • sour cream

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°. Grease a 9”-x-5” loaf pan with cooking spray and line with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a large bowl using a hand mixer, beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy, 2 to 3 minutes. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  3. Add bananas and vanilla and beat until combined, then add dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  4. Fold in chocolate chips and sour cream.
  5. Pour batter into prepared pan
  6. Bake until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 1 hour to 1 hour 10 minutes. Let cool 10 minutes in pan, then transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely.

Swedish Pancakes

Orgin: Sweden

Source: Kristi

Category: Breakfast

Recipe Ingredients

  • eggs
  • whole milk
  • all-purpose flour
  • granulated sugar
  • kosher salt
  • unsalted butter, melted
  • lingonberry jam
  • powdered sugar
  • fresh berries
  • vanilla
  • water

Directions

  1. In a blender, combine the eggs, milk, flour, sugar, salt, melted butter, vanilla and water. Blend until smooth. Let the batter rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat a 10-inch nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add a small amount of butter to the pan and swirl to coat. Pour 1/4 cup of the batter into the pan and swirl to coat the bottom of the pan. Cook until the edges of the pancake begin to brown and the top is set, about 1 minute. Flip and cook for another 30 seconds. Transfer to a plate and cover with a clean kitchen towel. Repeat with the remaining batter, stacking the pancakes on top of each other as you go.
  3. Spread each pancake with a thin layer of lingonberry jam and roll up. Dust with powdered sugar and serve with fresh berries.
